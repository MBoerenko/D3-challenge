Correlation Between Obesity and Poverty

The visualization above displays a negative correlation between income and obesity. There is a visible trend that suggests that the lower income states tend to have higher incidences of obesity. Arkansas, West Virginia, and Mississippi rank the highest in terms of obesity and lowest in income, while states such as Washington DC and Hawaii enjoy economic prosperity and a thinner population. It is possible that the correlation is due to higher unemployment, lower education levels, and irregular meals.

However, the trend is not absolute, there are some clear outliers. For example, there are states such as Maryland and Alaska with relatively high incomes and high obesity rates.
